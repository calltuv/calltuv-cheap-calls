Why international calls can still be expensive

Even though messaging apps and internet calling are widely available, many people still need reliable phone calls to international mobile or landline numbers. Costs can vary depending on the destination country, the type of number being called, carrier fees, connection quality, and whether the call uses the public phone network or an internet-based service.

The cheapest option is not always the best option. Some services are low-cost but unreliable, while others are convenient but expensive. The best choice depends on who you are calling, how often you call, and whether the person receiving the call has internet access.

Main ways to call abroad

Mobile carrier international plans

These are convenient because they work directly from your regular phone number, but they may include monthly fees, high per-minute rates, or limited destination coverage.

VoIP calling apps

VoIP apps often offer lower prices and app-based calling. They can be a strong option when both users have good internet access or when the app supports calls to regular phone numbers.

Calling cards

Calling cards can be affordable, but they may involve access numbers, PINs, connection fees, maintenance fees, or confusing rate structures.

International calling cost factors

Before choosing a service, compare the total cost of the call rather than only looking at the advertised per-minute rate. Important factors include:

  • The country you are calling
  • Whether you are calling a mobile number or landline
  • Connection fees or setup fees
  • Billing increments, such as per-minute rounding
  • Call quality and reliability
  • Whether the other person needs an app or internet access

FAQs

What is the cheapest way to call internationally?

The cheapest option depends on the destination country, call length, and whether the person receiving the call can use an internet-based app. VoIP services and international calling apps are often cheaper than standard mobile carrier rates.

Are calling cards still worth using?

Calling cards can still be useful in some cases, but users should check for connection fees, maintenance fees, expiration rules, and complicated dialing instructions.

Is VoIP better than regular international calling?

VoIP can be cheaper and more flexible, but call quality may depend on internet strength, device compatibility, and the destination being called.

Does the person I call need to install an app?

It depends on the service. Some apps require both people to use the same app, while others allow calls to regular mobile or landline numbers.

How should I compare international calling services?

Compare the destination rate, connection quality, ease of use, fees, billing increments, and whether the recipient needs internet access.
